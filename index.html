Social Profile Exchange Protocol

Social Network. Liberated.

SPXP is not a Social Network itself. It's an agreement on how independent actors can conjointly form a social network. It provides:


 Freedom

  • Any webserver can host profiles
  • Freed timeline (no algorithm or bubble)
  • Encourages implementation ecosystem
  • Choose your app to see profiles

 Privacy

  • Define who sees what
  • Own your content & data
  • Private posts end-to-end encrypted
  • Proven internet security standards

 Transparency

  • Open source
  • Human readable
  • Welcomes participation
  • Easy to learn and get started

Privacy

The makers of SPXP believe that privacy is a fundamental human right and the protocol was designed from the ground up to provide end-to-end encryption for all of its aspects. Consequently, if you choose to, every piece of information that's either stored on or in transition through an SPXP server remains completely unreadable for the server as well as third parties.

Asymmetric profile encryption

Under the hood, SPXP uses a private / public key pair to encrypt your profile. When you create a new profile on your mobile phone, the private key is automatically stored there. However, it is important to understand that your profile's integrity is bound to you (and noone else) posessing its private key. With this respect, an SPXP profile is very similar to a Bitcoin address. So make sure you store your private key in a secure place!

Open Source

SPXP is developed in the open on GitHub. Everybody can see its source code and we welcome participation very much. Do you like to tinker around and manually create your own profile or do you have an improvement suggestion? Head over to GitHub and check out our organization.

It's a protocol, not a server!

The network topoligy of the big social networks of our time typically looks like a star - one big server server with many many clients (e.g. mobile phones). SPXP couldn't be more different. It's a protocol that defines how servers and clients can talk to each other in a way that the resulting communication depicts what we commonly know as a social network. With this regards, it's topoligy is much similar to a mesh (like the the internet).

Choose your client (and host)!

Because it's a protocol, there's not the one internet address you need to go to or the one app you need to use. Instead, if you're interested and have some technical skills, you can host profiles yourself or write an app to view them. This allows for a wide variety of offerings and business models.

Own "the algorithm"

SPXP has the potential to make a real difference when it comes to "the algorithm". It's the mechanism that suggests content to you and usually tries to make you stay inside a social network for as long as possible. The reasoning is simple: the longer you stay, the more advertisements you see. To achieve this goal today's "algorithms" tend to show you more and more content similar to what you already saw. The resulting effect is often that views and believes you already had are amplified as it's getting harder to view differing content. Many refer to this as a "bubble" that's surrounding you and distorting your perception of the outside world.
The good news is, with SPXP, there is no "one algorithm". Instead, we're convinced there's going to be a wide variety of clients (apps) that differ - amongst other things - in the way they suggest content to you. Imagine clients that allow you to adjust the parameters of content suggestion. Or if you cannot find a client that suits your needs, go ahead and write your own!
